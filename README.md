# CS 230 Reflection on a Portfolio  - The Gaming Room

## Client and Software Requirements
The Gaming Room, a company that would like to expand their game Draw It or Lose It, which was initially a platform with Android-only capabilities, to a web-based application became the client in this project. They aimed at ensuring that the game would be available on a variety of devices including desktop and mobile browsers. The software had to be able to support multiple teams and players, to have unique names and only have a single instance of the game service with appropriate design patterns. The system was also required to be scalable, secure and efficient.

## Strengths in Documentation
One thing I did particularly well in this documentation was clearly explaining the system design and architecture. I effectively applied the concepts of object-oriented like inheritance and encapsulation within the domain model. I also appropriately used design patterns such as the Singleton pattern and Iterator pattern that assisted in enhancing the organization of the system and preventing duplication.

## Importance of the Design Process
The design document made me realize the significance of planning the coding beforehand. It facilitated the process of organizing ideas, defining system requirements and considering scalability and performance at an early stage. This helped enhance the general software design.

## Areas for Improvement
One aspect that I would change were the evaluation part because I would include more comparisons and explanation. I would also add diagrams to facilitate easier understanding of the design.

## Understanding User Needs
I understood the needs of the user based on the areas of accessibility, performance, and scalability. The consideration of user needs is significant as it makes a system usable, efficient and meets expectations.

## Future Design Approach
I have employed system analysis, design patterns and object-oriented design in designing software. Agile techniques and user feedback are also some of the techniques that I would apply in the future to further enhance the design.
